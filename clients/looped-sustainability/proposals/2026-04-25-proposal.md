## What We're Going to Build

### Build Phase — Months 1–3

**1. Material Research Agent + Trello**
This is the 20-hour problem. Right now, finding the right buyer for a new waste stream means hours of Google, ChatGPT, cold calling, and asking around. We'll build an AI research agent that changes that workflow entirely.

When a new opportunity card is created in Trello, the agent identifies likely end uses, surfaces nearby buyers and processors with contacts, and comments a prioritized "who to call" list — with indicative pricing — directly on the card.

**Input:** Material type + volume + location + any known constraints
**Output:** A ranked list of 15–25 potential buyers — processors, recyclers, manufacturers — with:
- Company name and description
- Contact name and phone number
- Geographic proximity and estimated freight cost
- Why they're a fit for this material

Your sales team picks up the phone. They don't spend the morning researching.

The agent will also go beyond your current broker relationships for freight — pulling from load boards and additional broker networks to give you a better spread on shipping costs before you quote.

**2. AP/AR Invoice Automation**
Right now, invoices land in your Gmail inbox and wait for Andrew. We'll build an automated pipeline that:
- Monitors your AP inbox continuously
- Extracts invoice data from attachments (PDFs, Excel)
- Validates amounts against C-Trade records and flags mismatches
- Prepares QuickBooks postings for review

Andrew stops manually touching invoices. Discrepancies surface automatically rather than silently accumulating.

**3. Workflow Audit & Automation Roadmap**
I'll map every major manual process end-to-end — freight quoting, account management, order tracking, document generation — and identify automation candidates by impact and risk. This produces the prioritized roadmap that guides Retainer Phase work. We're never guessing what to build next.
