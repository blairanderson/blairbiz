## What We're Going to Build

### Phase 1 — Quick Wins (Months 1–2)

**1. Invoice & AP Automation**
Right now, invoices land in your Gmail inbox and wait for Andrew. We'll build an automated pipeline that:
- Monitors your AP inbox continuously
- Extracts invoice data (vendor, amount, line items, due date)
- Routes and records into QuickBooks Online automatically
- Flags exceptions that need human review

Andrew sleeps again.

**2. Sales Call Transcription → Trello**
We'll set up Granola (or equivalent — no bot joining the call) on your team's machines so every sales conversation is automatically transcribed and summarized. Key details from each call — material type, volume, location, contact name — get structured and pushed directly into Trello as a properly formatted card. No more detail lost between the call and the board.

**3. Current Workflow Audit**
I'll map every manual process your team touches — scheduling trucks, confirming load pickups, generating documents, AR follow-ups — and build a prioritized automation roadmap for Month 3 and beyond.

---

### Phase 2 — Research Agent (Months 2–3)

This is the 20-hour problem. Right now, finding the right buyer for a new waste stream means hours of Google, ChatGPT, cold calling, and asking around. We'll build an AI research agent that changes that workflow entirely.

**Input:** Material type + volume + location + any known constraints
**Output:** A ranked list of 15–25 potential buyers — processors, recyclers, manufacturers — with:
- Company name and description
- Contact name and phone number
- Geographic proximity and estimated freight cost
- Why they're a fit for this material

Your sales team picks up the phone. They don't spend the morning researching.

The agent will also go beyond your current broker relationships for freight — pulling from load boards and additional broker networks to give you a better spread on shipping costs before you quote.

### Phase 3 — Ongoing (Month 4+)

After the initial build sprint, the engagement shifts to an ongoing fractional CTO model:
- Bi-weekly attendance at your sales meetings (or weekly — your call)
- Continuous identification of new friction and automation opportunities
- Technology vendor evaluation (including C-Trade replacement options when the time is right)
- AR automation, document generation, and client communication workflows as priorities emerge

The goal is simple: every time someone on your team says "I hate doing this manually," that becomes the next project.
