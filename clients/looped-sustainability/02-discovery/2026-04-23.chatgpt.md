## Automation Opportunities

1. **AP email -> invoice extraction -> C-Trade / QuickBooks reconciliation**
   Blair should lead with this. The client said this is the “bigger payoff,” the workflow is already centralized in one AP inbox, and the ROI is obvious: faster posting, fewer manual checks, less burnout, better cash control. Pitch an intake pipeline that reads AP email attachments, extracts invoice data, validates against load/job data in C-Trade, flags mismatches, and prepares/executes QuickBooks posting.

2. **Account-management copilot for load ops**
   This is the next strongest operational win because they named the exact tasks: truck scheduling, request confirmations, document generation, day-to-day follow-through. Pitch a lightweight ops layer that turns incoming requests into structured tasks, drafts confirmations, tracks status, and generates the standard documents around each load.

3. **Freight rate shopping and broker/load-board automation**
   This has strong enthusiasm and likely fast ROI because freight “makes or kills a deal.” Pitch an always-on quoting workflow that takes lane + material details from Trello/C-Trade, queries broker portals/load boards, compares options, and returns the best priced feasible move with timing coverage beyond the Macedonia work window.

4. **Research agent for outlet discovery and price guidance**
   This is the clearest sales-side concept from the call. Pitch a research workflow that takes a material card, identifies likely end uses, surfaces nearby buyers/processors, finds direct contacts/phone numbers, and returns a “who to call first” shortlist plus indicative pricing logic. Their own framing was: best options, pricing guide, then tee up the calls.

5. **Sales call transcription -> Trello enrichment -> coaching prompts**
   Lower complexity, fast trust-builder. They were receptive to Granola-style capture and already recognized lost detail in cards. Pitch automatic meeting/call summaries, extracted specs/questions, Trello card updates, and prompts like “ask about cardboard / plastic / storage / volume” on future calls.

6. **Lead-list building for cold outreach**
   This is useful but slightly lower priority because the team still believes phone works best and Blair himself framed AI calling as not ready. Pitch list-building only: target companies within distance constraints, role/contact enrichment, and call sheets for Tommy/Andrew/the sales team.
