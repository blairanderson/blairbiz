Phase 1 — First 90 Days

Three priorities. Highest value first.

The Build Phase backlog is maintained collaboratively — you and I adjust priorities week to week based on what's actually burning. These are the three agreed starting points, in priority order.

01
Cort's Priority

Material Research Agent + Trello

When a new opportunity card is created in Trello, the agent identifies likely non-obvious end uses for the material, surfaces nearby buyers and processors with contacts, and comments a prioritized "who to call" list with indicative pricing directly on the card. Sales reps get a research head start before they ever pick up the phone.

Their framing: "Finds our best options, gives us a pricing guide, and tees up exactly who we need to call." That's exactly what this builds.

02
Andrew's #1

AP / AR Invoice Automation

Monitors the AP email inbox, extracts invoice data from attachments (PDFs, Excel), validates amounts against C-Trade records, flags mismatches, and prepares QuickBooks postings for review. Andrew stops manually touching invoices. Discrepancies surface automatically rather than silently accumulating.

Why it's next: Fully internal — clients never see it. Highest daily pain for Andrew. Immediate, measurable relief.

03
Operations

Workflow Audit & Automation Roadmap

Document the major manual workflows end-to-end: freight quoting, account management, order tracking, document generation. Identify automation candidates by impact and risk. Prioritized roadmap that guides Retainer Phase work — so we're never guessing what to build next.

Freight quoting and account management ops are the natural next layer after Phase 1. The audit makes sure we sequence them correctly.

The Model

Fractional CTO. In practice, that means this.

Not project-by-project. An ongoing technical relationship where I hear where friction is and solve the right problems in the right order.

Weekly

Weekly Sync

30–45 minutes. Review what shipped. Hear what's burning. Adjust priorities. This keeps us aligned without a lot of overhead.

Monthly

Written Summary

End of each month: what was built, current status on each project, what's next. Easy to share with stakeholders or use as a record.

Ongoing

Slack / Async Access

Day-to-day questions, edge cases, something breaks — fast loop without scheduling overhead. Not on-call, but reliably responsive during business hours.

Retainer

Bi-Weekly Sales Meeting Attendance (Month 4+)

Once we're in the Retainer Phase, I attend your sales meetings every other week (or weekly by agreement). Hearing deals in real time is how I build tools that actually match your process — not a guessed version of it.

Always

You Own Everything Built

All automations, integrations, scripts, and documentation belong to Looped upon receipt of payment for the month in which they were delivered. No vendor lock-in to me. Full handoff package included at any transition point.

Investment

Predictable monthly rate. No surprise bills.

Third-party costs (API subscriptions, hosting, software) are passed through at cost with your approval — no markup. Out-of-scope work requires a written amendment before it starts.
